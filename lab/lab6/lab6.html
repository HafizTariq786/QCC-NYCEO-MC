What exactly is a favicon?

Favicons are small square images usually 16×16 pixels which are used by web browsers to show a graphical representation of the site being visited at the left side of the browser’s address bar. You have probably seen many favicons before, even if you don’t know what they are.









Below are just few examples to some favicons you may be fimiliar with (with respectable links).

How are favicons created?

Creating a website favicon is easy as pie. As a matter of fact, you don’t even need to be a designer to do that. Surely it helps if you are, as you can put your skills to work and create something that really stands out, but even the less tech-savvy of us can do it in about 5 minutes or less, using the right tools.

How did I create the favicon for this site?

I created the favicon for my site at the studio code . I went over the tutorial videos and able to understand how we use the binary language to create a favicon. To make my own favicon, I first set up the image width by lenth of exactly 32x32 with 3 bit per pixel. I then draw, my logo and fill up the pixel with that design and colors and my favicon was created exactly what I wanted it to be. After making a favicon, I just download my favicon
